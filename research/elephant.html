Elephant Skin-inspired Building Skin

Evaporative cooling through crack pattern engraved hygroscopic materials

This research presents an evaporative cooling screen inspired by African elephant thermoregulation, proposing a material-driven strategy to reduce mechanical cooling energy loads under hot conditions. Drawing from the thermoregulatory strategy of elephants, which retain water within cracked skin surfaces to enable evaporative heat dissipation, the building screen integrates hygroscopic materials with designed surface cracking to store moisture and reduce surrounding thermal stress through evaporation, framing the system as a climate-responsive architectural screen. The research follows a multi-scalar methodology: (1) an in-depth biological and anatomical analysis of African elephant cooling strategies informs their architectural translation; (2) hygroscopic materials are selected through material experiments evaluating structural and chemical stability, durability, and water retention capacity; (3) crack morphology is developed through a generative design process that maximizes moisture retention and inter-crack channeling; and (4) a structurally stable physical prototype is fabricated and tested under real climatic conditions to evaluate environmental performance. This work frames design modeling as a mediating process through which the latent thermodynamic behavior of hygroscopic materials is articulated and amplified.
